=== Life events research === Life events research investigates how significant life events, such as loss, trauma, or major life transitions, can impact mental health. It is one example of a longitudinal approach to social psychiatry. By following individuals over time, researchers can identify the long-term consequences of early life experiences and social factors on mental health. === Landmark studies === Several landmark studies have significantly contributed to the field of social psychiatry. ==== Faris and Dunham's Chicago study (1939) ==== This 1939 study, 'Mental Disorders in Urban Areas: An Ecological Study of Schizophrenia and Other Psychoses' was one of the first studies to link mental health issues with social and environmental factors, shifting the focus from purely biological explanations to sociological ones.
